Kākāpō
Critically Endangered

The Kākāpō is a large green parrot that lives in New Zealand. There are only a few left because they were hunted and their forests were cut down. They also have trouble surviving because animals like stoats and rats eat their eggs.

How can we protect it?

  • Keep them safe on predator free islands
  • Help them breed in special programs
  • Donating to groups that help save native birds

Threats

Many of New Zealand’s native birds are in danger because of:

  • Predators like rats, stoats, and possums that eat eggs and baby birds
  • Habitat loss when forests are cut down for farming or houses
  • Pollution and climate change, which make it harder for birds to survive
  • Birds didn’t evolve with these predators, so they don’t know how to protect themselves

How You Can Help

  • Keep your pets (especially cats) indoors at night
  • Plant native trees to give birds food and shelter
  • Learn more and teach others about NZ native birds
  • Donate to or join bird-protection groups
  • Spread the word through family and friends
  • Support your local environmental community
  • Dispose of rubbish properly so wildlife stays safe
